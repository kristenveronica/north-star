# The Living Family Record

**The conceptual blueprint for the Living Family Model's memory.** *What does North Star actually know about a family?*

Date: 2026-07-16 · This is not a technical document. It defines no databases, tables, schemas, or JSON. It describes **understanding, not storage** — what North Star comes to know about a family, imagined as if it had no implementation at all. It is the bridge between the philosophy above it and the substrate that will be built beneath it.

It sits at the base of the constitutional stack, downstream of all three:

- **The Constitution** — *who* North Star is (a guide that grows wise through shared history).
- **The Wisdom Model** — *how it discerns* what matters — what to attend to, keep, say, and forget.
- **The Observation Framework** — *how it speaks* what it has discerned.
- **The Living Family Record** *(this document)* — *what it holds* — the conceptual shape of everything it understands, and how each part lives, strengthens, and fades over time.

The three documents above answer *why* memory matters and *how* it should be handled. This one answers the question that must be settled before a single line of the substrate is written: **what is there to remember in the first place?**

---

## The one principle beneath everything here

> **The Living Family Model should not *merely* remember events. It should remember what events *mean*.**

That sentence governs everything below. A record of events alone is a database. An accumulation of *meaning* is a relationship. The difference is the whole product.

An event is *what happened*: a bridge was built, a conversation occurred, a trip was taken, a goal was met. Meaning is *what it revealed*: that this child moves toward challenge, that this family comes alive making things together, that these two siblings steady each other. **Meaning is what North Star reasons from** — it rises and becomes the primary material of the model's understanding, because meaning is the only thing that compounds.

But "not merely events" is not "delete the events." Meaning that cannot point back to what produced it is just an assertion — and a guide that cannot say *why* it believes something, cannot revise it, cannot weigh evidence against it, and cannot be corrected by a parent, is not trustworthy. So North Star keeps **both** — in two clearly separated layers, described next.

The Constitution names the same distinction from the other side — *accumulate understanding, not history.* This document holds the two together: understanding as the living, reasoning layer; history as the durable, traceable ground it stands on.

---

## The two layers: the Archive and the Understanding

North Star holds what it knows in two distinct layers, and keeping them separate is the most important structural decision in the entire model.

### Layer 1 — The Family Archive *(what actually happened)*
The durable, faithful record of source material: projects, reflections, uploads, milestones, and the conversations or notes a family chooses to keep. The Archive is **history, kept honestly.** It is not where North Star does its reasoning — a guide does not re-read every transcript to think — but it is never fabricated, never silently rewritten, and (subject to the family's own retention choices) never carelessly destroyed. It exists so that understanding can always be traced back to something real.

### Layer 2 — The Living Understanding *(what those events may mean)*
What North Star has come to *believe* the Archive reveals: interests, capabilities, relationships, culture, growth, preferences, challenges, emerging patterns. This is the layer the model actually reasons from. It is **provisional by nature** — always a current best interpretation, never a settled fact — and every piece of it points back to the Archive evidence that supports (or contradicts) it.

### How the two relate
Understanding **rises** from the Archive and becomes the primary material North Star thinks with. The Archive **recedes** from active reasoning — it does not sit in every context the AI considers — but it **remains**, traceable, so the model can always:

- **explain why** it believes something ("*I've noticed this because of these three projects*"),
- **revise** an understanding when the family changes,
- **recognise contradicting evidence** and hold it openly,
- **let a parent correct it** — *accurate · partly accurate · no longer true · incorrect · do not use*,
- and **preserve meaningful family memories** that deserve keeping in their own right.

The earlier language of this document — "release the record," "let the event fall away" — should be read in this precise sense: the *event leaves active understanding*, not that it is deleted. It settles into the Archive. What rises is meaning; what remains beneath is the evidence that meaning was earned. **Nothing true is thrown away; it is simply moved to where it belongs** — and what the family keeps, and for how long, is ultimately the family's choice, not the model's (see *Conversations & Reflections*, and the retention states in Part Three).

This separation is what makes the whole model trustworthy rather than merely clever: a system that only kept conclusions could never be questioned; one that only kept events would never understand. North Star keeps both, and keeps them apart.

## A shared vocabulary: the five lifespans

Before the domains, the vocabulary that classifies them. Every kind of understanding North Star holds has a natural lifespan. Naming five of them gives the memory substrate its most important instruction — *how firmly to hold a thing, and how readily to let it go.*

| Lifespan | Nature | How memory should treat it |
|---|---|---|
| **Permanent** | Bedrock. Effectively never expires. | Hold indefinitely. It may be *revised* when a person genuinely changes, but it never decays on its own. |
| **Slow-changing** | Evolves over years, not weeks. | Hold firmly between confirmations; revisit periodically; let it drift only with real, repeated evidence. |
| **Seasonal** | Belongs to a chapter — months to a year. | Hold firmly *now*; expect it to turn; release it gently when the season clearly ends, keeping only what it taught. |
| **Temporary** | True for now — days to weeks. | Hold lightly. Let it decay quickly unless reinforced. Its value is usually as *evidence*, not as a fact to keep. |
| **Momentary** | A single event or exchange. | Do not keep as history. Keep only if it *teaches* something that promotes into a durable layer above it. |

Two things about this ladder matter more than the labels:

**Understanding rises; history falls.** A momentary event ("Noah chose the harder bridge") is not kept as an event. If it teaches something, that *understanding* is promoted upward — into a Slow-changing capability, or a Permanent truth about his temperament — and the event itself is released. This upward promotion is the mechanism of *accumulating understanding rather than history*. It is the single most important behaviour the substrate must support.

**A domain is not monolithic.** Most domains hold elements at several lifespans at once. A child's identity holds a Permanent temperament *and* a Seasonal readiness. The class named for each domain below is its **centre of gravity** — where most of its weight sits — not a claim that everything inside it shares one lifespan. The substrate must classify *elements*, not just domains.

# Part One — What North Star knows

Seventeen conceptual domains, grouped into six strata by their nature — from the enduring frame of who a family is, down to the living stream of what is happening now. For each: **why it matters**, **how it changes**, whether it should **strengthen / weaken / expire**, whether it is **observed / told / inferred**, and **how it deepens understanding**.

---

## Stratum I — Who the family is *(the enduring frame)*

### 1. Family Identity & Structure — *Slow-changing (with Permanent facts)*
> The factual "who": who is in this family, and how it is shaped.

- **Why it matters:** Everything else hangs on it. You cannot guide a family you cannot name — its members, their ages, the structure (two parents, one, blended, multigenerational), faith, home base, family type. It is the frame every other understanding is hung upon.
- **How it changes:** Mostly stable, punctuated by significant events — a birth, a move, a marriage, a loss. Ages advance continuously and quietly.
- **Strengthen / weaken / expire:** Should not decay. Facts are *updated* on real events, never eroded by time. Some elements are Permanent (a child exists; a heritage); most are Slow-changing.
- **Observed · told · inferred:** Primarily **told** — a family states who they are. A little is inferred (an unstated rhythm of who is present when).
- **How it deepens understanding:** It is the skeleton. Without it, no observation can be anchored to a person or a place.

### 2. Family Values & Vision — *Permanent (slowly refined)*
> The "why" beneath everything — what this family is *for*.

- **Why it matters:** The Constitution's fixed point. Mission, motto, core word, educational philosophy, what they are raising their children *toward*. Every recommendation must bend around this; it never bends around a recommendation. It is the deepest, most defining thing North Star can know.
- **How it changes:** Rarely, and slowly. Values deepen and get articulated more clearly over years; they seldom reverse. When they do shift, it is a major event, not a drift.
- **Strengthen / weaken / expire:** Should **strengthen** — grow richer and more precisely understood with every year and every choice that confirms it. Never expires.
- **Observed · told · inferred:** Begins as **told** (elicited through the vision work), then continually **confirmed or refined by observation** — what a family actually chooses reveals what they actually value, which may be truer than what they first said.
- **How it deepens understanding:** It is the interpretive lens for everything else. The same behaviour means different things in a family that prizes independence versus one that prizes togetherness. Values are how North Star reads meaning.

### 3. Family Culture — *Slow-changing*
> The texture of how they actually live — the "how," beneath the "why."

- **Why it matters:** Values are the ideals; culture is the lived reality. The rhythms, the household habits, whether they learn hands-on or bookish, quiet or boisterous, planned or spontaneous; how they handle conflict, mark a win, move through a week. Guidance that ignores culture is merely *ideal* — correct and useless.
- **How it changes:** Gradually, as a family grows and its children age. A house of toddlers has a different culture than the same house of teenagers.
- **Strengthen / weaken / expire:** Slowly reinforced by repetition; slowly revised as life changes. Rarely expires outright — it *evolves*.
- **Observed · told · inferred:** Mostly **inferred and observed** — culture is rarely stated; it is seen in the pattern of how a family actually behaves over time. Some is **told** ("we're not morning people").
- **How it deepens understanding:** It is what makes a recommendation *fit*. Culture tells North Star not just what would help, but what this family could actually receive.

### 4. Traditions & Rituals — *Slow-changing (accretes; some Seasonal)*
> The specific recurring practices a family holds dear.

- **Why it matters:** Traditions are where a family's values become tangible and repeatable — the celebrations, seasonal markers, customs, the small sacred repetitions. They are among the most emotionally load-bearing things a family owns, and a guide that honours them (and suggests worthy new ones at the right moment) earns deep trust.
- **How it changes:** Accumulates. Traditions are begun, kept for years, occasionally retired. Many are Seasonal in *expression* (they recur each autumn) but Slow-changing in *existence*.
- **Strengthen / weaken / expire:** Should **strengthen** as they recur; **weaken** quietly if a family stops practising them (noticed, never nagged); a retired tradition becomes part of Shared History rather than being deleted.
- **Observed · told · inferred:** **Told and observed** — sometimes named directly, sometimes noticed by their recurrence.
- **How it deepens understanding:** Traditions reveal what a family chooses to protect and repeat — a direct window onto what they treasure.

## Stratum II — Who each child is becoming

### 5. Child Identity & Temperament — *Permanent (slowly revised)*
> Who each child fundamentally is, and how they need to be met.

- **Why it matters:** The deepest understanding North Star can hold about a person — the grain of their nature: how they meet the world, whether they need encouragement before challenge, how they respond to difficulty, where they are tender and where they are strong. This is what lets guidance be personal rather than generic. Every child is *a person becoming themselves, never a profile in a category* (Constitution §2).
- **How it changes:** Slowly, and it must be *allowed* to. A child is never finished; even a deep temperament can surprise. But it changes far more slowly than interests or moods — mistaking a phase for a temperament is a cardinal error (Wisdom Model, the ladder).
- **Strengthen / weaken / expire:** Should **strengthen and deepen** with years of evidence — but always **remain revisable.** It must never harden into a label that traps the child (Observation Framework: "an observation that hardens into a cage").
- **Observed · told · inferred:** Overwhelmingly **inferred**, slowly, from patterns across seasons. A little **told** by a parent who knows their child well. Never asserted from a single behaviour.
- **How it deepens understanding:** It is the highest-value, hardest-won layer at the individual level — the difference between guidance *for a nine-year-old* and guidance *for this child.*

### 6. Interests & Passions — *Seasonal (a through-line may become Permanent)*
> What currently captivates each child.

- **Why it matters:** Interests are the fuel of engagement — the fastest, most reliable path into a child's motivation. A project built on a live interest works; one built on a dead one does not.
- **How it changes:** Constantly, and at very different rates. A three-week dinosaur obsession and a six-year love of building are both "interests," and treating them alike is a failure. The art is distinguishing the passing fascination from the enduring passion.
- **Strengthen / weaken / expire:** Must be able to do all three. A fascination should **decay** if it stops appearing — *forgetting a phase is a feature of wisdom* (Wisdom Model). A repeatedly confirmed interest should **strengthen**, and a long, deep through-line can graduate into a near-Permanent characteristic.
- **Observed · told · inferred:** **Observed and told** — seen in what a child chooses, said by a child or parent. Confidence comes from *recurrence*, not from one enthusiastic afternoon.
- **How it deepens understanding:** The pattern of interests over time is itself an understanding — not "likes dinosaurs" but "is drawn, again and again, to how things work."

### 7. Capabilities & Growth — *Slow-changing (the trajectory is Permanent)*
> What each child can do, how ready they are to stretch, and how that is developing.

- **Why it matters:** This is Level 4 of the observation ladder — *how a person is changing.* It holds present competence, current confidence, and readiness for the next challenge. It is what lets North Star *prepare rather than react* (Constitution §4): sensing when a child is ready to move from following instructions to designing their own.
- **How it changes:** Present capability changes Slowly; *readiness* is more Seasonal, even Momentary (a child ready this month, wobbly the next); the *trajectory* — the record of growth across seasons — only accumulates.
- **Strengthen / weaken / expire:** Capability should **strengthen** and rarely regress. Readiness should be held **loosely and revisited often**. The growth trajectory should be **kept permanently** — it is one of the few genuine histories worth preserving, because a child's arc is a gift to show them and their parents.
- **Observed · told · inferred:** **Inferred and observed** from what a child actually does, across time. Readiness is inferred with humility and stated tentatively.
- **How it deepens understanding:** Growth is a *pattern of patterns* — the deepest per-child understanding after temperament, and the one most easily wronged by a system that freezes an old truth in place.

### 8. Interpretive Lenses *(optional, parent-chosen)* — *Permanent-as-stated, but always low-weight*
> Astrology, human design, personality frameworks a parent chooses to add.

- **Why it matters:** Some families find these languages meaningful, and North Star should honour that. But they carry a specific danger the Constitution names directly: they must be held as *tendencies to consider, never verdicts that define a child*, and kept **rigorously distinct** from what North Star has actually observed.
- **How it changes:** Essentially fixed once a parent supplies them (a birth chart does not change). What changes is only how much weight, if any, the family wants them to carry.
- **Strengthen / weaken / expire:** Should **never strengthen on their own** and never accrue confidence from repetition — that is exactly the trap. They stay a static, optional, clearly-labelled lens, permanently subordinate to observed reality.
- **Observed · told · inferred:** Purely **told**. Never inferred, and never allowed to masquerade as an observation.
- **How it deepens understanding:** At most, a gentle prompt for what *might* be worth watching for — never evidence, never a conclusion. Their contribution is deliberately capped.

## Stratum III — The bonds between them

### 9. Relationships — *Slow-changing (with Seasonal texture)*
> The web between people — because a family is not a set of individuals but a fabric of bonds.

- **Why it matters:** Level 3 of the ladder — *how people influence one another.* Each parent↔child bond, each sibling↔sibling bond, the whole-family dynamic. This is where North Star begins to understand a family *as a family.* Much of flourishing lives here: a shared adventure, two siblings who have been drifting, a bond ready to be nurtured.
- **How it changes:** Slowly at the core (a deep sibling loyalty), with real seasonal texture on the surface (a rough patch, a sudden closeness). Bonds shift as children age and life turns.
- **Strengthen / weaken / expire:** Enduring bonds **strengthen and deepen**; frictions should be held as **Seasonal and revisable**, never as a fixed verdict about two people ("they don't get along") that could become self-fulfilling.
- **Observed · told · inferred:** Mostly **inferred and observed** — who chooses whom, who engages longer with whom present. Some **told** by a parent.
- **How it deepens understanding:** It lifts North Star from understanding children to understanding a *family* — and unlocks the recommendations that strengthen connection, not just instruction.

---

## Stratum IV — The conditions of their life

### 10. Context & Resources — *Seasonal / Temporary*
> The real conditions that make guidance possible rather than merely ideal.

- **Why it matters:** Location now, travel, the *time actually available* (not the time an app wishes they had), the inventory and resources they already own before anything is suggested to buy, their finances held with care. This is what keeps recommendations *possible.* Ignoring it produces beautiful, unusable advice.
- **How it changes:** Frequently. This is the most changeable factual layer — a trip, a busy fortnight, a new resource acquired, a tight month.
- **Strengthen / weaken / expire:** Should **expire readily.** Last month's schedule and last year's inventory are actively misleading if kept. This layer must be *current or absent* — stale context is worse than none.
- **Observed · told · inferred:** **Told and observed** — stated by the family, and inferred from what they actually use and do.
- **How it deepens understanding:** It is the reality check on every suggestion — the difference between a guide that fits into a family's real life and one that adds to its burden.

### 11. Seasons of Life — *Seasonal (but leaves Permanent marks)*
> The larger arcs a family moves through.

- **Why it matters:** Above the week and the month sit the chapters: a new baby, a house move, a hard year, a child crossing into adolescence, a season of growing autonomy, a rite of passage on the horizon. Sensing these is how North Star *prepares rather than reacts* — offering the right thing *early enough to matter.*
- **How it changes:** By definition, in chapters. A season builds, holds, and passes — and the next one is different.
- **Strengthen / weaken / expire:** The *current* season should be held firmly, then **released** as it passes. But its *residue* — how the family came through it, what it revealed — should **promote into Permanent** Shared History and understanding. The season ends; its lesson does not.
- **Observed · told · inferred:** **Inferred and told** — sometimes named by a family, more often sensed by North Star from the accumulating pattern and offered gently.
- **How it deepens understanding:** It gives guidance *timing.* The same suggestion is wise in one season and tone-deaf in another; knowing the season is knowing when.

## Stratum V — What they reach for and wrestle with

### 12. Goals & Aspirations — *Seasonal (some enduring)*
> What the family and each child are working toward.

- **Why it matters:** Goals are direction. They tell North Star where a family *wants* to go, so its guidance can pull toward it rather than wander. Aspirations — the larger hopes — shape years of choices.
- **How it changes:** Goals complete, lapse, or are replaced on a Seasonal rhythm. Aspirations change far more slowly and shade toward Values.
- **Strengthen / weaken / expire:** A completed goal should **expire gracefully** — and what it *taught* (that this child finishes what they start; that this family follows through together) should promote upward. An abandoned goal should weaken quietly, and be noticed if the abandonment is itself a pattern.
- **Observed · told · inferred:** Mostly **told**, then **inferred** where a family's real, revealed goals differ from their stated ones.
- **How it deepens understanding:** Goals connect understanding to action — they are where "what would help this family flourish" becomes something specific to move toward.

### 13. Challenges & Struggles — *Temporary (the overcoming becomes Permanent)*
> What is hard right now — and what they have come through.

- **Why it matters:** A guide that only knows a family's strengths does not really know them. The current struggle — a subject a child is stuck on, a strained relationship, a hard season — is where support is most needed and most felt. And *a family that came through something hard should be met by a guide that remembers they did* (Constitution §2).
- **How it changes:** Current struggles are Temporary by nature — they resolve, ease, or transform. Some recur.
- **Strengthen / weaken / expire:** The *current* struggle should be held gently and allowed to **expire** as it resolves — carrying a struggle forward after it has passed is its own quiet harm. But the **overcoming** is Permanent: the fact that this family faced something and made it through is core to who they are and must never be forgotten.
- **Observed · told · inferred:** **Told and observed** — sometimes confided, sometimes noticed with care.
- **How it deepens understanding:** Struggles, and especially struggles overcome, reveal a family's resilience, their edges, and where they most need a guide walking alongside them.

## Stratum VI — The living record *(memory itself)*

*These four domains are not facts about a family — they are the accumulating record through which North Star comes to understand everything above. They are where raw history is turned into kept understanding.*

### 14. Observations — *Variable by level (this is the living asset)*
> What North Star has genuinely noticed — the distilled understanding itself.

- **Why it matters:** This is the asset. Everything else in the Record is *input*; Observations are the *understanding* North Star has actually formed from it — the patterns, relationships, growth, and flourishing it has come to see (the five-level ladder). Fully specified already in the [Observation Framework](observation-framework.md); named here because it is *where promoted understanding lands.*
- **How it changes:** Continuously. Observations strengthen, weaken, are refined, are replaced, and disappear as the family changes (Observation Framework: "Observations evolve").
- **Strengthen / weaken / expire:** All of these, by design — and at a rate set by their level on the ladder. A behaviour-level note is nearly Momentary; a flourishing-level understanding, once earned, is close to Permanent.
- **Observed · told · inferred:** Overwhelmingly **inferred** — an observation is, by definition, something North Star *concluded*, held with explicit supporting and contradicting evidence.
- **How it deepens understanding:** It *is* the understanding. This is the layer that makes a family feel known, and the one the whole substrate exists to grow and protect.

### 15. Shared History & Meaningful Moments — *Permanent (but ruthlessly curated)*
> The moments worth returning to — the family's story, kept.

- **Why it matters:** Milestones, celebrations, meaningful moments, challenges overcome, the family stories worth keeping. This is the one place North Star keeps *history* on purpose — because these specific events carry lasting meaning and returning to them is part of what makes it feel like a guide that *knows* them.
- **How it changes:** Only grows, and only slowly — because the bar to enter is high. *History only matters when it teaches or means something.* The vast majority of events never become Shared History; they pass through.
- **Strengthen / weaken / expire:** Kept **Permanently** once admitted — but admission is rare and deliberate. The discipline is not in keeping these; it is in *how few* qualify.
- **Observed · told · inferred:** **Observed and told** — some moments are named by the family as significant, some are recognised by North Star as worth holding.
- **How it deepens understanding:** It is the family's felt continuity — the difference between a guide with a memory and a guide with an archive.

### 16. Conversations & Reflections — *Momentary → distilled to durable*
> The weekly conversations and reflections — the richest input stream.

- **Why it matters:** The Weekly Conversation and family reflections are where a family speaks in its own voice — the single richest source of genuine understanding North Star has. But their *value* is not the transcript; it is what the transcript *reveals.*
- **How it changes:** Each conversation is a Momentary event. New ones arrive on a weekly rhythm.
- **Strengthen / weaken / expire:** What a conversation *taught* — an interest revealed, a struggle confided, a value expressed — should **promote upward** into the durable Understanding layers. The raw exchange itself leaves *active reasoning* — North Star is not building a surveillance log it re-reads endlessly — but whether the original is **kept in the Archive, kept only as a summary, used briefly then deleted, or excluded from memory entirely is the family's choice, not the model's.** This is the promotion principle at its most important and most ethically sensitive: the *meaning* rises to active understanding; the *record* is retained, summarised, or discarded strictly according to the family's stated retention wishes (see Part Three).
- **Observed · told · inferred:** **Told** in its raw form; the understanding drawn from it is **inferred**, carefully.
- **How it deepens understanding:** It is the primary engine of the whole model — the place where "what would help this family flourish" is most directly heard. But only if North Star listens for *meaning*, not for *data*.

### 17. Projects & Activities *(in-flight)* — *Temporary (the understanding gained is durable)*
> What is currently underway.

- **Why it matters:** Active projects are the family's present shared work — what North Star should be tracking, supporting, and building around *right now.*
- **How it changes:** A project is active, then it is done. Its status is inherently Temporary.
- **Strengthen / weaken / expire:** The project-as-a-task should **expire** on completion — it moves out of the live layer. What it *taught* is a different thing entirely, and is the whole subject of Part Two.
- **Observed · told · inferred:** **Observed and told** — created with the family, tracked by North Star.
- **How it deepens understanding:** A project is not understanding; it is an *occasion for* understanding. Its lasting value is entirely in what it reveals — which is exactly where this document turns next.

# Part Two — What to remember when a project ends

*This is the test case for the entire model. If North Star handles the end of a project correctly, it handles memory correctly.*

When a family completes a project, the temptation is to record the project. **North Star should record almost none of it.** The project — the title, the steps, the fact that it happened — is Temporary. It moves out of the live layer and, in most cases, out of memory entirely. The bridge stays in the child's portfolio as a made thing; it does not stay in the *understanding.*

What North Star should keep is the **understanding the project made visible** — and then promote that understanding *upward* into the durable domain it belongs to, letting the event that produced it fall away.

The difference, made concrete:

| North Star should *not* keep… | …it should keep the understanding, promoted to a domain |
|---|---|
| "Noah completed a bridge project." | "Noah, after a first attempt, *chose the harder design himself.*" → **Capabilities & Growth** (a step in his trajectory from following to designing) and possibly → **Child Identity** (he moves toward challenge, not away). |
| "The family did an outdoor build in June." | "This family is most engaged, and most collaborative, when they *make things together outdoors.*" → **Family Culture** and → **Relationships** (a flourishing-level understanding). |
| "Jett joined Noah's project." | "Jett stays engaged far longer *when Noah is involved.*" → **Relationships**. |
| "The project took three weeks and was completed." | "Noah *sees long projects through* — he did not abandon it when it got hard." → **Capabilities & Growth**, and a small deposit toward → **Child Identity**. |

Four principles govern this:

1. **Keep the lesson in Understanding; keep the event in the Archive.** The project is the occasion; the understanding is the keepsake. Once the understanding is promoted, the event leaves *active reasoning* — but it stays in the Archive as the traceable evidence for what was learned, so the belief can always be explained, revised, or contradicted. Promotion moves meaning *up*; it does not erase what happened.
2. **Promote to the right lifespan.** A single project yields *tentative* understanding — usually a Momentary observation that must earn its way up the ladder through repetition before it strengthens into a Slow-changing or Permanent truth. One bridge does not prove a temperament. The promotion is a *nomination*, not a coronation (Wisdom Model: "one behaviour is not a pattern").
3. **Most projects teach nothing new — and that is normal.** If a project merely reconfirms what North Star already understood, it should add a quiet increment of *confidence* to the existing understanding and keep nothing else. A model that extracts a profound new insight from every activity is not perceptive; it is over-reading. **Learning nothing new is the common, correct outcome.**
4. **Understanding may surface as a rare, quiet observation.** Occasionally — and only when it clears the five tests — the understanding gained is worth reflecting back to the family (Observation Framework: "occasionally after a project is completed"). Usually it simply deepens the model's private understanding and waits.

The same logic applies to every domain in Stratum VI: a conversation, a reflection, a milestone, a completed goal. In each case the raw event is Temporary or Momentary; the *understanding* it reveals is what promotes and endures. **North Star accumulates the second and lets go of the first.** That is the whole discipline of a memory that grows wiser instead of merely fuller.

# Part Three — How each lifespan should be remembered

The five lifespans are not a taxonomy for its own sake. They are the instruction set for the memory substrate — telling it, for any piece of understanding, how firmly to hold it, when to revisit it, and how readily to let it go. This is the behaviour the substrate must implement.

### Permanent — *hold and deepen*
*Values & Vision · Child Identity & Temperament · Shared History · growth trajectories · struggles overcome.*
Held indefinitely. **Strengthens** with confirming evidence; **never decays on its own.** May be *revised* only on real, repeated evidence that a person or family has genuinely changed — and even then, gently, because a change to something Permanent is a significant event, not a routine update. The danger here is the opposite of forgetting: **hardening.** Permanent must never mean *fixed*. Even bedrock is revisable when the person changes.

### Slow-changing — *hold firmly, revisit periodically*
*Family Identity · Culture · Traditions · Capabilities · Relationships.*
Held with confidence between confirmations, but **revisited on a slow cadence** — seasons, not weeks. Drifts only with genuine, repeated evidence; a single contradicting event should not move it, but a sustained new pattern should. The failure modes are symmetric: updating too eagerly (chasing noise) and updating too reluctantly (freezing an old truth).

### Seasonal — *hold now, expect the turn, release gently*
*Interests · Relationships' surface texture · Context · Seasons of Life · Goals · readiness.*
Held firmly *for the current season*, then **released as the season clearly passes** — with its residue promoted upward if it taught something durable. The key behaviour is *graceful expiry*: a season that has ended should stop shaping guidance, but should not be abruptly deleted, and what it revealed should be preserved. Carrying a past season forward as if it were present is a common and damaging error (an ended struggle, an outgrown readiness).

### Temporary — *hold lightly, decay quickly*
*Current struggles · in-flight projects · this week's context · fresh, unconfirmed interests.*
Held loosely and allowed to **decay fast unless reinforced.** Its value is almost always as *evidence* feeding something above it, not as a fact worth keeping in its own right. If a Temporary item keeps recurring, that recurrence is itself a signal to promote it to a longer lifespan. If it does not recur, it should simply fade — no deletion ceremony required.

### Momentary — *promote the meaning; the record is the family's to keep or not*
*Single behaviours · individual conversation turns · one-off events.*
**Not kept in active Understanding.** A momentary event is evaluated for what it *teaches*: if it nominates or reinforces an understanding above it, that understanding is what persists in the reasoning layer; the raw event does not. Whether the event itself is retained in the Archive is a separate, family-governed question — most momentary source material need not be kept at all, and a model that hoarded every behaviour and utterance would be a surveillance log, not a guide, and a betrayal of the trust that lets a family share in the first place. The default instinct here is restraint: *most momentary events teach nothing, and their rightful end is to leave no trace* — unless the family has asked for the source to be preserved.

## The single instruction to the substrate

Every mechanism the memory substrate will eventually implement — how it writes, strengthens, weakens, expires, and promotes — exists to serve one behaviour, which is the whole of this document compressed to a sentence:

> **Hold each understanding for as long as it remains true and useful, strengthen it as evidence repeats, keep every belief traceable to the Archive that produced it, promote what is taught into the layer where it belongs, and let the rest recede from active reasoning — retained, summarised, or discarded as the family chooses — so that what North Star *thinks with* is not a family's history, but its understanding of who they are, always able to point back to why.**

When a decision about *what to remember, how firmly, and for how long* is unclear, return here — and above here, to the Wisdom Model, which decides whether a thing was ever worth holding at all.
